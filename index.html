SEOUL- The Biggest City of South Korea

Overview

Seoul, the capital of South Korea, is a vibrant city that blends modernity with rich history.
Located in the northwestern part of the country, it has been the heart of Korean politics, culture, and economy for centuries.
Seoul is a must-visit for its impressive skyscrapers, ancient palaces, and buzzing markets.

Attractions

  • Gyeongbokgung Palace

    • One of Seoul’s most iconic landmarks, this grand palace dates back to the 14th century and showcases traditional Korean architecture.

  • N Seoul Tower

    • Located on Namsan Mountain, N Seoul Tower offers panoramic views of the entire city. It’s a popular spot for couples to hang love locks.

  • Bukchon Hanok Village

    • A picturesque neighborhood of traditional Korean houses (hanoks), where visitors can experience Seoul’s historic charm.

  • Myeongdong Shopping Street

    • A shopper’s paradise, known for its beauty products, clothing stores, and street food stalls

  • Changdeokgung Palace

    • A UNESCO World Heritage site, this palace is known for its beautiful gardens and integration with nature.
